Peripheral-to-Thalamic Neural Modeling Framework

Computational neuroscience · Network dynamics · Systems modeling

Currently, myself and Dr. Alexander Neiman are working to expand TouchSim-generated afferent activity into a multi-stage processing framework that includes thalamic relay neurons and their downstream transformations. The goal is to model how synaptic integration, temporal filtering, and population-level coding shape tactile information as it ascends toward cortex. We hope to characterize the information transfer, spike-timing structure, and emergent dynamics across stages of this multilayered system.

Vibrotactile Coordinated Reset (vCR) Modeling — MS Thesis

Neural simulations · Signal processing · HPC

Vibrotactile Coordinated Reset (vCR) is a non-invasive stimulation strategy designed to counteract the pathological synchrony observed in Parkinson’s disease by delivering carefully timed tactile pulses that disrupt aberrant rhythmic firing. Using TouchSim, we model how mechanoreceptive afferents—SA, RA, and PC fibers—respond to different stimulation sequences and how these responses propagate through peripheral pathways. This framework allows controlled exploration of phase-locking behavior, synchrony dynamics, and population-level response patterns across the digits under a variety of vCR parameter regimes.

Previous Projects ▶

Diffuse Reflectance Spectroscope (Clinical Prototype) — Miami University REU

Clinical instrumentation · Bench testing · QA/QC

Diffuse reflectance spectroscopy is a non-invasive optical technique that measures tissue health by analyzing how light scatters and is absorbed beneath the surface. Clinical prototypes of these instruments must demonstrate stable illumination, accurate calibration, and reliable spectral readout before being used in patient-facing environments. My work focused on validating the performance of a prototype spectroscope and identifying the factors that affect repeatability and signal quality.

AFM Coarse Approach (KoalaDrive) Troubleshooting — Marietta College

STM/AFM · Materials Science · Failure analysis

Atomic force microscopes rely on a coarse-approach mechanism—often a piezoelectric “walking” motor—to bring the probe close to the sample with sub-micron level control. When this mechanism fails, stalls, or exhibits stick–slip behavior, the entire imaging system becomes unusable regardless of the quality of the scanning hardware. My work centered on diagnosing the failure mode of a KoalaDrive-style coarse approach system and exploring materials and mechanical strategies to improve reliability.

Thin Film Electron Transport — Marietta College

UHV systems · Electron Transport · Surface Science

This experiment aimed to probe free-electron transport in thin gold films by introducing controlled scattering centers on the surface. We measured the film’s resistance before and during adsorption of dimethyl sulfide, whose molecules act as random point scatterers for conduction electrons. By tracking resistance changes under these conditions, we evaluated how surface adsorbates perturb electron mean free paths and alter transport behavior.
