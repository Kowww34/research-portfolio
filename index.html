Overview

I’m happiest at the intersection of science and engineering, where ideas have to hold up both in a model and on a workbench. My personal and professional interests span physics, biology, and neuroscience, with a particular focus on how quantitative models and real-world experiments inform one another. I enjoy building and testing models, coding large simulations, and developing practical tools that make complex systems easier to understand. Equally, I love prototyping and have a strong knack for working with my hands, designing experiments, and troubleshooting. My recent work centers on modeling neuronal dynamics and information processing, combining computational analysis with a curiosity-driven approach to understanding biological systems.

Current Project — Peripheral-to-Thalamic Neural Modeling Framework

Computational neuroscience · Network dynamics · Systems modeling

  • Extending TouchSim outputs into a multi-stage neural processing pipeline incorporating thalamic relay neurons.
  • Modeling synaptic integration, temporal filtering, and population-level coding as afferent signals ascend toward cortex.
  • Building modular analysis tools to explore information transfer, spike timing relationships, and emergent network behavior.

Master’s Thesis — Vibrotactile Coordinated Reset (vCR) Modeling

Neural simulations · Signal processing · HPC

  • Developed large-scale TouchSim-based pipelines to simulate SA/RA/PC afferent responses under vCR stimulation protocols.
  • Analyzed firing statistics, phase-locking behavior, and synchrony patterns using PSD, coherence, and dimensionality-reduction methods.
  • Automated data aggregation and visualization workflows suitable for publication, presentations, and replication.

Marietta College — AFM Coarse Approach (KoalaDrive) Troubleshooting

Coarse approach · Piezoelectric motors · Failure analysis

  • Troubleshot the KoalaDrive piezoelectric motor used for AFM coarse approach, diagnosing loss-of-motion and sticking behavior during approach.
  • Investigated alternative material stacks and lubrication schemes (e.g., MoS₂ layers, composite interfaces) to mitigate suspected vacuum welding at critical junctions.
  • Helped design and evaluate methods to characterize motion (video tracking, interferometry concepts, capacitance-based modeling) and guide future redesigns.
